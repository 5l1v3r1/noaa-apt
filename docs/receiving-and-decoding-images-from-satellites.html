Why NOAA satellites

The easiest satellites to receive images from are some NOAA satellites from the POES constellation, we are interested on NOAA 15, NOAA 18 and NOAA 19. From now on I'm going to call them NOAA satellites like everyone but we can only receive three of them, that's because they use "old technology", we do not need expensive equipment.

Some facts about these NOAA satellites:

  • They have a low orbit, at about 800km above the surface of the Earth, really low compared to geostationary satellites at 35,786km. Because of this the satellites move quite fast and are available above the horizon only for 10 minutes and go away.

  • The orbit is polar, this means that they go from north to south or from south to north. Also means that they can cover all the surface of the Earth eventually.

  • The satellites have no memory, they send the images at realtime. they can cover all the surface of the Earth eventually.
